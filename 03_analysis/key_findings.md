# Key Findings

## Main Finding

Discounting becomes a profitability risk at the 30%+ level, especially for Tables.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Profit loss increases at higher discount levels
Profitability begins to decline more consistently once discounts reach approximately 30% or higher.

### 2. Tables are the lowest-performing sub-category
Tables generate the lowest overall profit, making them a critical area for performance review.

### 3. Tables are frequently discounted at risk levels
Tables are often discounted near the 30% threshold, compounding profitability issues.

### 4. Revenue does not equal profitability
High sales do not always translate to strong profit margins across sub-categories.
